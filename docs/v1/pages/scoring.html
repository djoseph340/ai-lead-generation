Scoring Logic

How Scoring Works

Each lead starts at 0 points. Points are added based on signals in the form submission. The total determines where the lead goes and how it's classified.

Scoring Rules (v1.0)

Signal Condition Points
Industry Healthcare +30
Company Size 200+ employees +25
Message Content Contains "AI" +20
Contains "Automation" +20

Note: A lead can accumulate points from multiple signals. For example, if a lead is from Healthcare AND has 200+ employees AND mentions "AI", the score is 30 + 25 + 20 = 75.

Priority Assignment

After scoring, leads are assigned a priority tier:

High Priority

Score ≥ 80

Leads that meet or exceed the qualification threshold. These go directly to the Leads sheet for sales.

Medium Priority

Score 50–79

Warm leads that show some fit but didn't hit the threshold. Classified with tier and reasons on Unqualified sheet.

Low Priority

Score < 50

Poor fit. Minimal match with criteria. Stored for reference but unlikely to convert.

Example Calculation

Incoming Lead:

  • Name: Donald Smith
  • Company: Baylor Inc (250 employees)
  • Industry: Healthcare
  • Message: "Looking for AI-powered supply chain automation"

Score Breakdown:

  • Healthcare industry: +30
  • 250 employees (≥200): +25
  • Contains "AI": +20
  • Contains "Automation": +20

Total: 95 points → High Priority (≥80) → Qualified

Tuning Rules

Rules live in the n8n Code node. To adjust:

  1. Edit the scoring rule (e.g., change Healthcare from +30 to +40)
  2. Redeploy the workflow
  3. New leads use updated rules immediately

No model retraining, no data science needed. This is the advantage of rule-based systems.
